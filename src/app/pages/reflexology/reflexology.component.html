What is reflexology?

Reflexology:

  • relieves physical and emotional tensions
  • improves blood and nerve supply
  • helps balance and normalize body functions to optimize your energy level

Reflexology is a science based on the principle that there are reflexes on both hands and feet corresponding to each part, gland and organ of the body.

The goal of reflexology is to release blockages and congestion that can be found around the 7200 nerve endings of the foot and the 3000 nerve endings of the hand.

This ancestral practice goes back to about 2300 B.C. as some very ancient Egyptian papyrus show. It was rediscovered at the beginning of the 20th century by Dr William FitzGerald. What he called in 1913 “zone therapy”, that divided the body in 10 zones, allowed him, by pressing specific reflexes, to normalize the corresponding organs functions and to decrease the pain.

Later, Eunice Ingham, a physiotherapist, with Dr Joe Shelby Riley’s help, continued Dr FitzGerald’s work by designing a map of the locations of the reflexes corresponding to the different organs and body parts on both feet and hands. She also wrote several books to share her reflexology experiences. Her nephew, Dwight Byers, then took over by creating and leading the International Institute of Reflexology®. That institute’s purpose is still today to promote and teach reflexology according to the Ingham method.

So, how does it work?

Each organ and body part is connected through a huge network of nerves to a point on the foot and the hand. When circulation slows down, because of, for example, an illness, wearing unadapted shoes or a lack of physical activity, small crystals tend to accumulate and create blockages on the nerve endings. These are uric acid crystals that are mainly final products of the breakdown of proteins supplied to the body through the daily food intake.

Using a specific massage technique, the reflexologist is able to crush these crystals so that they can be dispersed, sent to the blood circulation or lymphatic system and then excreted by the kidneys.

By stimulating these blockage zones, reflexology helps the body relax, function better and find a good balance level.
